Aug 31 – Sep 7

Sicily & the Aeolian Islands

MG and dad join for the Aeolian loop, heading home Sep 4 from Vulcano.

Mon Aug 31 In transit — overnight flight from Canada

Overnight flight from Canada. Most of this day is in the air — no activities planned.

Tue Sep 1 Land CTA → drive to Milazzo

Land at Catania (CTA) in the morning, collect the rental car, drive straight to Milazzo (~2h north). After the long flight, keep it easy: check in, short walk up to the Castello di Milazzo for views of the Aeolians on the horizon if there's energy. Espresso at Bar San Filippo on the seafront. Early dinner, early night — hydrofoil to Panarea tomorrow morning.

Lodging: Hotel Riviera Lido — breakfast right on the beach.
Wed Sep 2 Milazzo → Panarea

Morning hydrofoil Milazzo → Panarea (~1h30). Panarea is the smallest and most exclusive of the Aeolians — whitewashed houses, black-rock coves, no cars. Hike to Cala Junco and explore the Bronze Age village ruins at Punta Milazzese. Dinner at Hyccara.

Lodging: ✅ Eunymos Panarea (booked).
Thu Sep 3 Panarea → Stromboli + Sciara del Fuoco sunset

Morning hydrofoil Panarea → Stromboli (~30 min). Beach at Ficogrande (black volcanic sand). Sunset: boat tour to the Sciara del Fuoco — watch lava cascade into the sea as the sun drops. The definitive Stromboli experience.

Lodging: ✅ Hotel Miramare (booked).
Fri Sep 4 Stromboli → Vulcano (MG & dad head home)

Optional morning: guided Magmatrek summit hike before departure. MG and dad take their ferry home today. Afternoon hydrofoil Stromboli → Vulcano (via Lipari). Evening: sulfur mud baths (Piscine di Venere), swim at Spiaggia dell'Asino, aperitivo at the port.

Lodging: Therasia Resort — ✓ Booked.
Sat Sep 5 Vulcano → Milazzo → Etna winery

Optional early morning: Gran Cratere hike before the hydrofoil. Drive south to Palmento Costanzo on Etna's northern slopes — 2-night winery stay.

Lodging: Palmento Costanzo.
Sun Sep 6 Gole dell'Alcantara + Taormina

Morning: drive down to the Gole dell'Alcantara — dramatic basalt lava gorges carved by the Alcantara River, walls up to 25m high. Walk into the gorge, wade in the cool turquoise water. Bring water shoes.

Afternoon: continue to Taormina — Teatro Greco with the volcano backdrop. Corso Umberto, granita stop, swim at Isola Bella. Return to the winery for sunset dinner.

Mon Sep 7 🎉 Isabella's engagement party — Catania

Drive to family (~45 min). Full day with family. Engagement party this evening.

Lodging: Grand Hotel Villa Itria.

Sep 8 – Sep 10

Cefalù · Palermo · Scopello

Tue Sep 8 Drive to Cefalù

Drive northwest from Catania (~2h). Explore the medieval old town: Norman Duomo (Byzantine mosaics), the Lavatoio medievale, and a climb up La Rocca for sweeping views. Sunset stroll along Cefalù beach.

Lodging: Hotel Kalura (own bay, pool + sea access) or Hotel Le Calette (boutique, private cove).
Wed Sep 9 Drive to Palermo · full city day

Drive west (~1h). Full day in Sicily's chaotic, beautiful capital: Vucciria and Ballarò markets, Cattedrale di Palermo, Palazzo dei Normanni / Cappella Palatina. Lunch at Spinnato on Via Principe di Belmonte. Dinner: Ferramenta, a stylish trattoria near Piazza Olivella.

Lodging: La Terrazza B&B.
Thu Sep 10 Scopello / Riserva dello Zingaro hike

Day trip west (~1h) to Scopello — a tiny fishing hamlet built around a 16th-century tonnara. Walk down to Cala Bianca: turquoise water, dramatic rock stacks. Hike into the Riserva Naturale dello Zingaro — Sicily's first nature reserve, a 7km coastal trail with hidden coves. Bring water, trail shoes, snorkeling gear. Early night before the morning flight.

Sep 11 – Sep 13

Florence

Fri Sep 11 Fly Palermo → Florence · arrive & explore

Morning flight PMO → Florence. Land, drop bags at the apartment. Walk straight into the historic center: cross the Ponte Vecchio, through to Piazza della Signoria and the Loggia dei Lanzi — an open-air sculpture gallery, entirely free. Coffee at Gilli or Paszkowski on Piazza della Repubblica.

Afternoon — Uffizi Gallery (timed-entry ticket). Botticelli's Birth of Venus and Primavera, Caravaggio, the long corridor of Renaissance masterpieces. Book a slot online in advance to skip the line.

Late afternoon: walk up to Piazzale Michelangelo for the best panoramic view in the city. If there's energy, San Miniato al Monte just above is quieter and even more beautiful. Dinner: Trattoria Cammillo or Buca Mario.

Sat Sep 12 Florence at your own pace

Early morning — Galleria dell'Accademia (open from 8:15am). Michelangelo's David in person — go right at opening to beat the crowds. 45 min–1 hour is enough.

Then: Brunelleschi's Cupola — climb the 463 steps through the narrow passage between the inner and outer shells of the dome, 360° views of Florence from the lantern at the top. Then wander to San Lorenzo market — leather stalls, ceramics, the covered food market upstairs. Lunch: All'Antico Vinaio on Via dei Neri.

Afternoon: deep dive into Oltrarno — Via dello Sprone, Piazza Santo Spirito, leather workshops on Borgo San Jacopo, bookbinding and ceramics shops. Bardini Gardens for greenery and a hilltop view without the Boboli crowds. Aperitivo: La Terrazza rooftop bar of Hotel Continentale — best view of Ponte Vecchio in the city. Early dinner, early night.

Sep 14 – Sep 17

Tuscany Road Trip

Mon Sep 14 Chianti drive · Antinori tasting · Panzano lunch → Castello di Fonterutoli

Pick up rental car in Florence. First stop: Antinori nel Chianti Classico in Bargino — striking architecture cut into the hillside, tasting of three signature wines. Continue to Greve in Chianti — Piazza Matteotti, Enoteca Falorni. Lunch: Antica Macelleria Cecchini in Panzano. Arrive at Castello di Fonterutoli (Mazzei Wine Resort, Castellina in Chianti) by early evening — check in, estate wine tasting, dinner on site or in the medieval hamlet.

Lodging: Castello di Fonterutoli — Mazzei Wine Resort ✓ Booked. us.mazzei.it
Tue Sep 15 Siena morning → Montalcino → Castello Banfi tasting

Morning: drive to Siena (~20 min from Fonterutoli) — Duomo (inlaid marble floor), Piazza del Campo, Enoteca Italiana for a pre-lunch glass. Afternoon: drive south to Montalcino for a Brunello di Montalcino tasting at Castello Banfi — hilltop castle, terrace views.

Lodging (3 nights, Sep 15–17): ✅ Corte Francigena.
Wed Sep 16 Val d'Orcia — the Gladiator landscape

Ridley Scott filmed Maximus running through the wheat fields here — the Val d'Orcia in September looks exactly like the film: golden light, rolling hills, lone cypress trees, absolute silence. Drive slowly. Stop often.

Morning drive to Pienza — walk the Corso Rossellino, Duomo terrace views, pecorino from the cheese shops. Lunch: Latte di Luna. Drive back along the SP146 — the most scenic road in Tuscany. Pull over at the Podere Belvedere viewpoint.

Afternoon — see how you feel: optional detour to Abbazia di Sant'Antimo, a breathtaking 12th-century Romanesque abbey alone in an olive grove. Or head back for a long afternoon at the pool and an early dinner.

Thu Sep 17 Montepulciano · Porta di Bacco · recharge afternoon

Morning: Montepulciano — medieval hilltop city, home of Vino Nobile. Browse the medieval corso, visit Piazza Grande, drop into a cellar for a tasting. Lunch: Porta di Bacco. Afternoon: no agenda — wander, pool, aperitivo in town, early dinner. Wind-down day before Rome.

Lodging: ✅ Corte Francigena (3rd night).

Sep 18 – Sep 21

Rome

Fri Sep 18 Saturnia hot springs en route · arrive Rome · Pantheon · Trevi

Leave Montepulciano ~9am. Drive to Saturnia (~1h15) for the morning at Cascate del Mulino — free natural hot spring waterfall pools over white travertine terraces. Arrive early before crowds build, bring water shoes. Leave by 1pm, drive to Rome (~2.5h) — in the city by mid-afternoon. Pantheon, Trevi Fountain (go back after 9pm when lit and quieter), Piazza Navona aperitivo. Dinner: Armando al Pantheon or Roscioli.

Lodging (Sep 18–21): Terlie Tomacelli ✓ Paid.
Sat Sep 19 Ancient Rome · Colosseum · Forum · Palatine

Colosseum + Roman Forum + Palatine with a guided underground/arena floor tour. Lunch in Monti — La Carbonara or Pastificio Guerra. Afternoon: Capitoline Museums or a Caravaggio walk. Dinner: Salumeria Roscioli or Trattoria Da Cesare al Casaletto.

Sun Sep 20 Vatican · Sistine · St. Peter's · Trastevere

Morning: Vatican Museums + Sistine Chapel + St. Peter's Basilica — earliest entry slot. Lunch: Bonci Pizzarium. Afternoon: Trastevere — cobblestone alleys, gelato at Fior di Luna, Santa Maria in Trastevere. Final Roman dinner: Pierluigi or Felice a Testaccio.

Mon Sep 21 Fly home from FCO

Transfer to Fiumicino. Final espresso. Fly home. Arrivederci, Italia.

Practical Notes

Good to Know

☀️ Weather

28–32°C in Sicily & Rome, low 30s Tuscany, cooling by Sep 17–21. Sea at its warmest; September much less crowded than August.

🚗 Driving

Rental car Sep 1 (CTA) for Milazzo, returned after hydrofoils. Pick up again Sep 14 Florence through Sep 18.

🚧 ZTLs

Florence, Rome centro storico, Siena, Montepulciano, and Pienza all have restricted traffic zones. Get your hotel to register your plate.

♨️ Saturnia

Cascate del Mulino is free. Bring water shoes — travertine is very slippery. Arrive by 9:30–10am before crowds build; leave by 1pm.

🌿 Zingaro Reserve

No food or drink sold inside. Bring 2L+ water each, snacks, trail shoes, snorkeling gear.

💶 Cash

~€200 for Aeolian boat tours, small trattorias, and Sicilian street food.
